**Project funded under the Regional Programme ERDF (FESR) 2021–2027 of the Friuli Venezia Giulia Region**

**Co-funded by the European Union – European Regional Development Fund (ERDF)**

---

## Context

Energy systems are undergoing a profound transformation, driven by decentralization, digitalization, and the integration of renewable energy sources.  

In this context, smart grids and microgrids generate large volumes of heterogeneous data, which are often difficult to integrate and effectively use.  

This fragmentation limits the ability to make timely decisions, optimize performance, and develop advanced predictive models.

---

## The project

The **Smart Grid Data Fabric** project aims to develop an open platform for managing and analyzing smart grid data.  

The goal is to transform heterogeneous data into reliable and actionable information to support operational, economic, and energy planning decisions.  

The solution reduces data preparation time and enables predictive digital twins and optimization strategies based on machine learning.

The project builds on a prototype already validated in a laboratory environment (TRL5) and evolves it towards a solution demonstrated in a relevant operational environment (TRL6), ensuring practical applicability and industrial transferability.

---

## What makes the project innovative

- **Semantic data integration**  
  A common model that enables interoperability between data from heterogeneous systems (such as batteries, photovoltaic systems, smart meters, and electric mobility).

- **Reduced time-to-value**  
  Simplified installation, configuration, and usage enable rapid access to value from data.

- **Open and modular architecture**  
  Based on open standards, with no vendor lock-in.

- **Computational efficiency and serverless models**  
  The use of lightweight, event-driven architectures, combined with serverless components, optimizes computational resource usage, improving scalability and operational sustainability.

---

## Expected impact

The project contributes to:

- improving the efficiency and reliability of energy networks  
- facilitating the integration of renewable energy sources  
- reducing data management costs and time  
- supporting research, innovation, and industrial applications  

In the medium term, the solution will enable new approaches to managing energy infrastructures, making them more sustainable, resilient, and data-driven.
